Transport

  1. Walk in the old town. There is a lot of history crammed on every meter square of the city. When you leave, there will still be one jewel of a building you haven't seen. And a reason to come back.
  2. Taxis are expensive. They will use a meter but charge a lot. The official taxis are white. If you call a taxi, it will also charge for the trip to you.
  3. Use the tram or bus to visit Mondello Beach.Busses are quite reliable and quick after 8pm and bring you closer to the old town than the tram.

Eating

  1. "Coperta" means tip in Italian. It covers the service charge and the cost of washing cuttlery and plates. It is usually a fixed amount (approx 2euro). In this way you never ned to worry how much tip you should leave and he good part: it is not a percentage. Also make sure to keep your cutlery or you pay more.
  2. Try the original arancino: a ball of fried rice with an inside filling - great lunch snack if you are on the rush and very tasty. To my opinion the cheese and spinach one is the best.
  3. The Canolli is Sicily's most famous desert : a small wrapped pastry that is filled with sweet ricotta cream and pistachios, but can also contain other ingredients.
  4. Fish in Palermo is very good. Try the sardines and sword fish, but check if they are in season for best taste and quality.
  5. Good restaurants to try:
    • Bisso Bistrot
      Address: Via Maqueda, 172A, 90134 Palermo PA, Italy

      Open 9am - 11 pm;
      Closed Sunday

      Tip: Reservation for dinner is a must, otherwise you may not get a table
    • Trattoria Ai Normanni
      Piazza della Vittoria, 25, 90100 Palermo PA, Italy

      Open 12pm-4pm and 6.30pm-12am;
      Sunday Open 12am -12pm

      Tip: double check if open in August
    • Al fondaco del Conte
      Piazza Conte Federico, 24, 90134 Palermo PA, Italy

      Open 7.30am - 10.45pm, reduced Sat, Sun, Tue
      Closed Monday

      Tip: don't let yourself be tricked by appearance. Restaurant looks old fashioned. But food is amazing.

Visit

  1. Make sure to double check opening hours of the places you want to visit as they have sometimes unusual timetables and sometimes may be closed for service.
  2. Carry a bottle of water with you at all times.
  3. If lucky during the summer months, book a piano concert in open air in Palermo.
  4. Try the seasonal cactus fruit in August from a street vendor
  5. The Town Hall is free to visit and offers a great view of the Fontana Pretoria as well as some insight into the modern local history.
